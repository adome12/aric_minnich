History of apple juice production

  1. The production of apple juice has a long history that dates back to ancient times. However, the modern apple juice industry began in the late 19th century, when technology improved and allowed for mass production. The first apple juice factory was opened in the United States in 1867, and by the early 20th century, the industry had expanded globally. Today, apple juice is a popular and common beverage consumed worldwide, with production and consumption increasing annually.

How we grow our apples

  1. Apples grow on deciduous trees in areas with cool climates. They start as small, flowering buds in the spring and then develop into fruits by late summer or early autumn. As the apple grows, it goes through several stages, starting as a small, green fruit and eventually turning red, yellow, or green depending on the variety. Apples need abundant sunlight, water and proper soil nutrients to grow. Once ripe, they can be gathered by hand or shaken from the trees,then sorted, cleaned, and packaged for sale or in our case given to our factory to make apple juice which we then sell
